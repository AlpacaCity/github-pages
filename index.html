🧬 The Mysterious Alpaca Gene

Alpacacinos‘ genes are fascinating. They determine the various traits Alpacacinos have and make every one of them unique.

  • Each Alpacacino has 12 different traits group.
  • 10 of the 12 traits are discoverable and are directly linked to our appearances and energy level, while the other 2 remain undiscovered and thus unknown to us.
  • Among the 10 discoverable traits, each carries 4 genes and only the dominant one will determine the appearance and energy level.
  • However, the 3 secret genes could be passed on to their babies when you breed them. So don't be surprised when you find the babies look different from their parents! 😉

⚡ Energy is important

Energy is very important.

  • Every single Alpacacino was born with a different level of energy, which is influenced by genes and generation number.
  • Each of these 10 traits, depending on the dominant gene, has an energy level of 1/ 5/ 10 or, in some rare cases, 15.
  • The energy level Is a sign of how much yield one Alpacacino can generate when you let them manage your assets. Higher the energy level, the higher the yield.

👼 Breeding Rules

Breeding is the key to grow the yield of your assets. Since Alpacacinos are a genderless, there is no gender limitation to the breeding process. However, the only rule is that each Alpacacino cannot breed new-borns with their parents or kids.

Alpacacinos also have different generations.

  • Every Alpacacino has a GN (Generation Number) which determines the breeding fees and energy levels.
  • In general, Alpacacinos with higher GN will have higher breeding fees and higher energy levels.
  • Our GN starts at 0 and Alpacacinos can breed across generations.
  • The baby's GN will be the minimum GN of either of the parents plus one. For instance, if mummy alpaca has a GN of 6 and daddy alpaca has a GN of 3, the baby alpa's GN will be 3+1=4.
